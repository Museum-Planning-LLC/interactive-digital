# Shared Ground

**Catalog entry** · Digital Exhibits · **full-room immersive**

## Summary

Full-room Digital Exhibits experience: **the same place, different stories**. Multiple narratives in one space — moving apart, the archive diverges; moving together, material that belongs to **both** histories at once can surface. Not a history lesson; does not assign content by visitor identity.

## Interaction model

- **Social geometry:** distance, direction, and velocity between bodies in space drive what appears
- **Plural archives:** content organized by community / emotional register, not only chronology
- **Convergence:** moments where overlapping or contested memory can surface when groups approach
- **Requires multiple participants** for the full intended behavior (single visitor = partial read)

## Form factor

| Attribute | Specification |
|-----------|----------------|
| Tier | Full-room immersive |

| Audio | Ambient field keyed to relational state |

Each **instantiation** uses **local archive** and the same underlying interaction logic. Cities / sites are content packages, not separate products.

## When to reference (menu / conversation)

- History, civil rights, or local museums where **multiple communities** share place
- Repositioned gallery where **shared authority** matters more than a single curatorial voice
- Study flagged **reactivation** of a room suited to communal, presence-driven experience

## Lineage (conceptual)

In conversation with spatial interaction works (e.g. Snibbe *Boundary Functions*, Utterback *Shifting Time*) — Shared Ground departs toward **convergence without Voronoi division**, and **relationship between bodies** navigating the archive rather than one body controlling time alone.
